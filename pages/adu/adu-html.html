What Is an ADU

A second home
on your property

An accessory dwelling unit is a smaller, independent residential unit on the same lot as a single-family home. ADUs can be detached structures in your backyard, attached extensions, or converted garages.

California's updated ADU laws have streamlined permitting, making it faster and more affordable to add a unit to your property.

ADU Types

Four ways to build

01

Detached ADU

Standalone unit in your backyard. Maximum privacy and flexibility in design.

02

Attached ADU

Extension connected to your existing home. Shares a wall but has its own entrance.

03

Garage Conversion

Convert your existing garage. Most affordable option, starting at $75K.

04

Above-Garage ADU

Build a second story above your garage. Keeps parking while adding living space.
